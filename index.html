Experience

At age 16, Zachary Hayworth made countless calls to professional sports teams seeking an arena job on game nights. After receiving no interest, he attempted to buy collections of sports cards and re-sell them individually over the internet, but quickly lost money. When he recognized that online consumers were constantly seeking rare and outdated items, he came up with the concept to identify the world's oldest product and make it readily available by using the newest internet technologies and e-commerce platforms. After purchasing a small quantity of prehistoric fossils, he uploaded several product listings from his local library and sold out of his inventory within an hour. Realizing he was able to secure steep price discounts on overstock of imperfect quality, he began acquiring lower-grade fossils in high volume through tradeshows, excavation organizations, and various other channels. In the years that followed, the business eventually evolved into a leading wholesaler of fossilized commodities, supplying dozens of businesses and thousands of customers around the world.

Following his exit from ownership, Zachary launched Hayworth & Co., a consulting and advisory service to guide both individuals and businesses on leveraging their strengths, identifying new opportunities, and improving in their respective fields. He also became a minority owner of several other ventures in the e-commerce and specialty commodities space.

Since founding his first business, Zachary continued to maintain his lifelong passion for sports. He recently launched an athlete advisory firm and is now a certified agent with the National Basketball Players Association. Over time, numerous professional athletes have sought him out for advice and consultation on a wide range of matters. Zachary has attended hundreds of National Basketball Association games and can frequently be found at major sports and entertainment functions across the United States. He has also served as a long-time assistant to the New Jersey-based Tom Coughlin Jay Fund Foundation, an organization overseen by former National Football League coach and current executive, Thomas Coughlin.

Education & Research

Zachary Hayworth received his Bachelor of Science degrees in business administration and entrepreneurship with honors from Rowan University in Glassboro, New Jersey. He later obtained a Master of Business Administration with a concentration in management from the University of Massachusetts, where he graduated with highest distinction and a 3.97 grade point average. He also pursued further research focused on business and communications at several universities, including Columbia University in New York City.

Currently, Zachary is a master's candidate at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina, where he pursues a specialized course sequence focused on advanced business strategy, sports law, and managerial issues in sports. At Duke, as well as independently, Zachary has conducted extensive research and produced numerous reports and papers focused on athlete representation, financial literacy for athletes, and emerging issues in the sports industry. His advisor is Dr. Paul Haagen of Duke Law School.
