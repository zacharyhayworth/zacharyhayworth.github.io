Experience

Zachary Hayworth has widespread experience as a consultant, advisor, representative, business manager, and entrepreneur. A native resident of northern New Jersey, Zachary Hayworth often works out of nearby midtown Manhattan in New York City. With clientele across the United States, he has also worked out of Florida, North Carolina, and Las Vegas, Nevada.

As a high school student, Zachary created a venture that sold prehistoric fossils through major e-commerce platforms. The company eventually grew to become a leading wholesaler of fossilized commodities to clients in over twenty countries. Following his years as an entrepreneur, Zachary represented the world’s leading supplier of prehistoric fossil jewelry, where he negotiated and secured contracts with some of the most well known theme parks, entertainment complexes, luxury resorts, museums, and aquariums.

Zachary is also a minority owner of and advisor to BKB, Inc., the independent export division of India-based Haileyburia Tea Estates Ltd., and represents the company in the North American market. Spread over 3,000 acres, the company employs over 1,000 people and produces nearly 4 million pounds (1.8 million kilograms) of black tea annually.

Zachary has also been a long-time assistant to the Tom Coughlin Jay Fund Foundation, an organization overseen by the former National Football League coach and current executive, Thomas R. Coughlin.

Education & Research

Zachary Hayworth received his Bachelor of Science degrees in business administration and entrepreneurship from Rowan University in New Jersey, with highest honors. He also holds a Master of Business Administration from the University of Massachusetts, which was accompanied by highest honors and a 3.97 grade point average. He is currently pursuing an additional master’s degree at a major research university, where his interdisciplinary curriculum is tailored to topics in advanced management, sports business, sports law, and communication.

Through his academic programs, as well as independently, Zachary’s research concentrations have included topics in sports, entertainment, and technology. He also conducted research at Columbia University in New York City, where he examined topics on strategy, management, and communication within various organizations and industries.
