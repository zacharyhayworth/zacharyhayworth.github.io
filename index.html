About

Zachary Hayworth has widespread experience as a consultant, advisor, representative, business manager, and entrepreneur.

As a high school student, Zachary created a venture that sold prehistoric fossils through major e-commerce platforms. The company eventually grew to become a leading wholesaler of fossilized commodities to clients in over twenty countries. Following his years as an entrepreneur, he has represented the world’s leading supplier of prehistoric fossil jewelry, negotiating and securing contracts with some of the most well known theme parks, entertainment complexes, luxury resorts, museums, and aquariums.

As part of his ongoing endeavors, Zachary is a minority owner of and an advisor to the independent export division of India-based Haileyburia Tea Estates Ltd., and represents the company in the North American market. Spread over 3,000 acres, the company employs over 1,000 people and produces nearly 4 million pounds of black tea annually.

Zachary has also been a long-time assistant to the Tom Coughlin Jay Fund Foundation, which is overseen by former National Football League coach and current executive, Thomas R. Coughlin.

A native resident of northern New Jersey, Zachary Hayworth often works out of nearby midtown Manhattan in New York City. Additionally, he has worked out of Florida, North Carolina, and Las Vegas, Nevada.

Education & Research

Zachary Hayworth received his Bachelor of Science degrees in Business Administration and Entrepreneurship from Rowan University in New Jersey, with highest honors. He also holds a Master of Business Administration from the University of Massachusetts, which was accompanied by highest honors and a 3.97 grade point average.

Through his academic programs, as well as independently, Zachary has conducted in-depth research on topics in sports, entertainment, technology, and communication. In addition, he conducted a year of research at Columbia University in New York City, where he examined topics on strategy, management, and policy within various organizations and markets.

He is currently pursuing a second master’s degree at Duke University in North Carolina, where he pursues an interdisciplinary curriculum that focuses on sports business, sports law, and topics in advanced management.
