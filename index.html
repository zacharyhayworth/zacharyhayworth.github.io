About Zachary Hayworth, Founding Partner

Zachary Hayworth formed The Hayworth Group after a longtime career as an entrepreneur. As a high school student in northern New Jersey, Zachary was fired from his first job at a local retail store due to poor performance. Unable to secure another employment opportunity, he decided to test the marketability of different products using the emerging e-commerce platforms of the mid-2000s. Upon realizing that consumers frequently sought rare items, he created several product listings for prehistoric fossils. When his test inventory sold out within hours, he began to focus on the venture full-time after school and on weekends. Zachary soon began to negotiate and secure steep price discounts on subpar grade fossils in high volume, which he began to offer to businesses rather than individual consumers. In the years that followed, the business evolved to become a leading wholesaler of fossilized commodities for major resorts, amusement parks, zoos, and other ventures in the entertainment and tourism space.

Following his exit from ownership, Zachary launched The Hayworth Group to guide both individuals and organizations on leveraging strengths and identifying new opportunities. He continues to maintain ownership holdings in ventures operating within specialty commodities, e-commerce, and sports management.

Education & Research

Zachary Hayworth has studied and conducted research at five different universities. Among his academic credentials are his Bachelor of Science degree in business administration and entrepreneurship with honors from Rowan University in Glassboro, New Jersey, which he completed in under three years. He later obtained a Master of Business Administration with a concentration in management from the University of Massachusetts, where he graduated with highest distinction.

Additionally, Zachary holds a master's degree from Duke University in Durham, North Carolina. At Duke, his academic interests and research focused on advanced management, legal issues sports, and the representation and marketing of athletes. Zachary's thesis advisor was Dr. Paul Haagen of Duke Law School.
