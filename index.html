Experience

Zachary Hayworth has extensive experience as a consultant, advisor, entrepreneur, and business manager. His clients operate across various industries including professional services, entertainment, e-commerce, and nonprofit.

As a high school student, Zachary launched a venture that sold prehistoric fossils through major e-commerce platforms. The company eventually grew to become a leading wholesaler of fossilized commodities to clients in over twenty countries.

Subsequent to operating his own company, Zachary became a long-time agent for the world’s leading supplier of prehistoric fossil jewelry. He has negotiated and secured contracts with some of the world’s largest theme parks, entertainment complexes, museums, aquariums, and luxury resorts.

Zachary is also a minority owner of and an advisor to BKB Inc., the independent export division of Haileyburia Tea Estates Ltd., and handles the company’s interests in the North American market. The company operates three tea estates— Haileyburia, Semni Valley, and Chinnar—spread over 3,000 acres in Elappara, Kerala, India. Employing over 1,000 people, the company produces 1.8 million kilograms (approximately 4 million pounds) of black tea annually.

Zachary has been a long-time assistant to the Tom Coughlin Jay Fund Foundation, which is overseen by former National Football League coach and current executive, Thomas R. Coughlin. The organization provides support to families battling childhood cancer.

A native of northern New Jersey, Zachary Hayworth often works out of nearby midtown Manhattan in New York City. With clients located throughout the United States, he has also worked out of several different areas, including Florida and Las Vegas, Nevada.

Education

Zachary Hayworth received his Bachelor of Science degrees in Business Administration and Entrepreneurship from Rowan University in New Jersey, which he completed with highest honors. Additionally, he holds a Master of Business Administration from the University of Massachusetts, which he completed with highest honors. He is a lifetime member of Beta Gamma Sigma, the international academic honor society for top business school students.

Through his academic programs, as well as independently, Zachary has conducted extensive research on topics in sports, entertainment, technology, and organizational communication. He also conducted a year of postgraduate research at Columbia University in New York City, where he examined strategy, management, and policy issues within the communications industry.

He is currently pursuing a second master’s degree at Duke University in North Carolina, where he pursues coursework in communications, sports law, sports business, and advanced management.
